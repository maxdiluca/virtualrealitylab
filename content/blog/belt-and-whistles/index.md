Imagine walking through a dark room: you slow down, extend your hands, and feel for furniture before you bump into it. VR users face a similar problem — they are effectively blind to their physical surroundings. When the virtual world does not align with the real one, immersion breaks the moment a user clips through a virtual wall or, worse, hits a real piece of furniture they could not see.

Research has shown that violations of physical rules in VR measurably reduce immersion, while physical coherence increases it. To address this, we developed a battery-powered, wireless **haptic belt** with six embedded voice coils that delivers directional feedback around the wearer's waist.

The belt provides two kinds of feedback:

- **Sharp pulses** on collision, signalling contact with a virtual object.
- **Gradually intensifying signals** as the user approaches an obstacle, giving them time to react.

In testing, the belt improved navigational plausibility without hindering performance during high-stress gaming tasks. Use cases we explored include:

- Rendering the sway of a virtual bridge under the user's feet.
- Signalling approaching hazards before they appear in the user's field of view.
- Alerting users about real walls when they step backwards out of their tracked play area.
